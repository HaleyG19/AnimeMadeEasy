Pre-war Anime (1923 - 1938)

The pre-war period in Japan between 1923 and 1938 was a very difficult time for Japanese animation. Damage from the Great Kanto Earthquake of 1923 destroyed many anime studios and made it nearly impossible to turn a profit from the production of anime. Between low funding, limited pay, and very few people willing to do the work, the future of the anime industry looked grim.

Japanese animators also experienced competition from foreign producers like Disney that continued to make more films and profits than the Japanese could keep up with. Foreign studios had colour, sound, and had big corporations on their side, all of which their Japanese counterparts struggled to obtain.

However, anime continued to be made during this period, albiet in smaller numbers and at lower quality than desired. The cel animation technique and the celluloid it required was too expensive for many production companies and so Japanese animation continued to use outdated and low tech alternatives which held them back.

Nevertheless, several firsts were achieved during this period, including the creation of the first talkie anime made by Kenzo Masaoka entitled The World of Power and Women (Chikara to Onna no Yo no Naka) in 1933, and the first entirely cel animated film made by Mitsuyo Seo entitled The Dance of the Chagamas in 1934.
