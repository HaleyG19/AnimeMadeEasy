The Evolution and History of Anime

From the time it began in the early 1900s to over a century later, the anime industry has been kicked down, battered and bruised more times than can be counted, yet time and again the industry has managed to rise again and captivate the hearts and minds of millions.

Starting out with rudimentary techniques involving paper cutouts and chalk, with showings at only select theaters throughout Japan, the anime industry of today is now a highly computerized, massively popular, worldwide phenomena that shows little sign of slowing down anytime soon.

From Activity Photo (Katsudo Shashin), 1907, to Neon Genesis Evangelion, 1995, and Astro Boy to One Punch Man, 2015; anime has been carefully sculpted by the masterful hands of brilliant creators for over a century, into the magnificent medium through which Japanese artists can tell us stories of love and adventure, heartbreak and betrayal, friendship and loyalty, and so much more.

So get ready, this history is a long one, but it is by no means a boring one.
