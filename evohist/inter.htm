Paving the Way (1960 - 1979)

Prior to this period, for people in Japan to view animation they had to go to movie theaters. After 1961, however, this was no longer the case. Anime was finally brought to television in 1960 with the broadcast of the film Three Tales and this increased the medium's fanbase exponentially.

The following year Instant History (Otogi Manga Calendar) began being released regularly and is credited with being the first anime series broadcast on Japanese television.

In 1961, after leaving the Toei Animation studio, artist Osamu Tezuka established his own studio called Mushi Production (Mushi Pro). In the years to follow Tezuka and his studio would produce some of the most famous anime from the period including Astro Boy (Tetsuwan Atom) in 1963, which would be a massively influential anime for years to come. Also of note from the studio were the series Kimba the White Lion (Janguru Taitei) in 1965, and Princess Knight (Ribon no Kishi) in 1968.

Astro Boy (Tetsuwan Atom), 1963

The 1970s also saw the rise of many popular anime even though the industry faced major competition from other television mediums. Notably, the boxing anime Tomorrow's Joe (Ashita no Joe) began airing in 1970, Lupin the Third (Rupan Sansei) and its later sequels recieved its first installment in 1971, and the mecha genre tore onto the scene with shows like Space Battleship Yamato (Uchuu Senkan Yamato) in 1974 and Mobile Suit Gundam (Kido Senshi Gandamu)in 1979.

Tomorrow's Joe (Ashita no Joe), 1970

Ultimately, this period secured anime's spot in Japanese popular culture and paved the way for genrations to come.
