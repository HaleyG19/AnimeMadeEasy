Anime Phrases To Know

Although it's not entirely necessary for you to know these phrases by heart, it never hurts to have a few of these Japanese expressions in your back pocket if you want to impress your fellow anime fans one day.

Again, the list below is by no means comprehensive, and definitely won't make it so you can watch anime without subtitles or dubbing, however, it will give you a little taste of the Japanese language and you may even be able to point out some of the phrases while you watch your favourite shows.

Common Anime Phrases

arigatou - word for "thank you" in Japanese.

chigau - commonly said when something is wrong. In English it means "No way!" or "Don't be ridiculous!"

doko - in English it means "Where." "Doko?"

gomennasai - Japanese term for an apology. "Gomennasai" means "I'm sorry."

hidoi - means to be severe or harsh. In English we would say "How terrible!" or "That's harsh!"

ii - Japanese word for "good" or "that's great!"

itadakimasu - directly translates to "I humbly recieve," but is more commonly used at mealtimes to say "let's eat" or "thanks for the food." In Japan this is said before every meal.

itai - means something hurts or is painful. In English we say "ouch!"

konnichiwa - Japanese greeting. In English it means "Hello."

kudasai - the polite way to ask for things. In English it's a magic word meaning "please"

nani - common expression of confusion. "Nani?" means "What?"

ohayo gozaimasu - daily formal greeting that means "Good morning"

oi - used the same way as the British use it; as a way to interrupt someone or something. "Hey!"

oishii - Japanese term used to express delight, especially regarding food. In English it would be "delicious"

okiari - used to welcome someone back. Commonly used to welcome someone home after work or school. "Welcome back."

omae wa mou shinderu - popular meme phrase from the anime series Fist of the North Star. Basically means "you are already dead." Often used as an insult.

sugoi - way to express that you are totally blown away by something or are in cplete amazement. Means "awesome" or "great."

suki da - used to express ones affection for another. In English we say "I like you,"

sumimasen - less formal way of apologizing. The English equivalent is "excuse me."

tadaima - response to someone welcoming you home. "I'm home."

urusai - in English it means "noisy" but is used in Japan as a phrase meaning "Shut up!"

yatta - expression meaning "Hooray!" or "I did it!"
