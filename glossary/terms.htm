Anime Terms to Know

There are many terms the anime community uses to describe shows, characters, and pretty much everything else. Sometimes the words are in Japanese and other times they are in English; either way the terms used are certainly hard for newcomers to understand and remain confusing even for some of the most diehard fans. The list below is by no means comprehensive, but hopefully it gives you a good starting point towards better understanding this terribly mismatched vocabulary anime lovers have created.

Terms that describe Characters

Baka - expression that someone is stupid or an idiot. "Baka!" Term is excessively used by the character Asuka in Neon Genesis Evangelion

Bishounen - used to describe "pretty boys" that have feminine features. Example: Victor from Yuri!!! on Ice

Chibi - something small and cute. Example: Reborn from Katekyo Hitman Reborn!

Chuunibyou - "8th grade syndrome," people older than middle school age that believe they have special powers but obviously don't. Example: Rikka from Love, Chunibyo and Other Delusions!

Dandere - character archetype that is quiet, shy, and unthreatening. They often wear glasses and love to read and usually only open up around their close friends or love interest. Example: Shiraishi from Yamada-kun and the Seven Witches

Deredere - overly cheerful and hyper character type. Constantly seen smothering the people they like with affection. They could get punched in the face and still instantly revert to their cheerful personality. Example: Usagi from Sailor Moon

Gaki - used to describe someone who is immature or a punk/ brat. Example: Joe from Tomorrow's Joe

Honorifics - we don't do this in English, but in Japanese people use honorifics/ suffixes to address people depending on familiarity, age differece, and respect

  • -chan : typically used for children but is also used by people who have known each other since childhood as a term of endearment. Mostly used when addressing females.
  • -kun : a more familiar way of addressing someone, typically male friends or relatives.
  • -san : used to address someone of equal or of close social standing, usually for someone you are less familiar with. In English we would use Mr., Miss, Mrs., etc.
  • -sama : super formal way of addressing someone, indicates a great deal of respect.
  • -senpai : used to address someone who is your senior in school. Upperclassman. Can be from the same social class as you but are superior due to their grade level.
  • -kohai : used to address underclassmen or one's junior, they are inferior to you in grade level.
  • -sensei : used to address a teacher

Kuudere - character archetype that is emotionless and distant. They can come off as very blunt and cynical on the outside, but are really just marshmallows on the inside and care a lot about their close friends and love interest. Usually they wear a blank expression and have a very flat, monotone voice. Example: Inori from Guilty Crown

Moe - used to describe characters that are super cute, innocent, vulnerable and childlike. These characters make others feel the need to protect them. Example: Honey from Ouran Highschool Host Club

NEET - "Not in Education, Employment, or Training." Refers to a useless member of society that does not work, go to school or do anything other than stay inside all day and play porn games, read manga, and watch anime. Sometimes this can be caused by mental disorders such as anxiety or depression. The Japanese look down on these people harshly. Example: Satou from Welcome to the NHK

Otaku - a subculture that describes people obsessed with a certain aspect of popular culture (typically anime) and lacking in social skills. Example: Kousaka from Genshiken

Weeaboo/ Weeb - English term used to describe someone who is so obsessed with Japanese culture and anime that they emulate it in their real lives.

Tsundere - super common character archetype that treats the people they like poorly beacsue they don't know how else to react. When called cute by someone they like they can get violent, flustered and angry. Famous for saying "It's not like I like you, or anything..." Example: SO MANY, Haruhi from The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya

Yandere - super scary character type. Trick you into thinking they are sweet and innocent then they reveal their true nature which is dark and obsessive. They often stalk the ones they love and get violent with those that try to get in the way. Range between being super shy to downright insane. Example: Yunno from Future Diary

Yankee - a delinquent. Example: Oga from Beelzebub

Terms that describe genres

Ecchi - anime that contain sexual scenes such as "panty shots" and cleavage without actually showing anything explicit. Example: High School DxD

Harem - anime where the protagonist is surrounded by 3 or more people that are attracted to them. Example: Sword Art Online

Hentai - Japanese word for "pervert" but also the genre for anime porn.

Josei/ Seinen - anime aimed at older women and men between the ages 18 and 30. Contain more mature themes than other anime. Example: March Comes In Like a Lion

Mahou - Japanese word for magic. Genre typically contains "magical girls." Example: Magical Girl Madoka Magica

Mecha - genre with tons of giant armoured robots. Example: Macross series

Shoujo/ Shounen - anime aimed at young girls and boys. Explore themes of friendship, loyalty, and romance. Examples: Banana Fish, One Piece

Yaoi/ Yuri - anime that focuses on relationships between same-sex partners. Examples: Classmates (Yaoi), Bloom Into You (Yuri)

Terms for everything else

Bakemono - in English this means monster.

Cosplay - this is the practice of dressing up as a fictional character, typically from anime, video games, or manga

Engrish - slang term used to describe the misuse of the English language by Asian people. Quite racist but also very funny to hear when anime characters pretend to know how to speak English. Example 1, Example 2

Fan Service - scenes in anime that are intentionally added to please viewers, for instance "beach episodes"

Kawaii - Japanese word for cute.

Manga - Japanese comic books/ graphic novels. Anime are often adaptations of popular manga.

Seitokai - Japanese word for student council. Many anime have characters that are in the student council. Example: Seitokai Yakuindomo

Seiyuu - Japanese word for a voice actor.
