Common Anime Genres

Like any television medium, anime has an endless amount of genres to pick from. Many times anime fall into more than one category and you may find you enjoy a show even if it is categorized under a genre you typically dislike. Below is a list of the most common anime genres and some examples of anime that can be classified under them.

Action - anime that are jam-packed with intense, adrenaline pumping scenes that keep you captivated and on the edge of your seat. Generally contain lots of fighting and battle scenes that are highly engaging and hype you up. Examples include Baccano! and One Punch Man

Adventure - anime that contain a quest with lots of travelling or a journey to a specific destination. Characters rarely stay in one place for too long and are always working towards some major end goal. Often this genre overlaps with others as it is so versatile. Examples include Spice and Wolf and Kino's Journey

Comedy - if you don't laugh then it isn't comedy. Okay, that's not entirely true but really that is the main point of this genre so if you don't enjoy wacky characters constantly getting up to hijinks and shenanigans then this isn't the genre for you. Examples include Asobi Asobase: Workshop Of Fun and Beelzebub

Drama/ Tragedy - this genre will likely make you cry if you are succeptible to such things. Anime from this genre tug at your heartstrings until they break and make you feel so much for the characters that their pain becomes your own. Not for the faint of heart. Examples include Golden Time, Your Lie in April, and Anohana: The Flower We Saw That Day

Slice of Life - a very chill and relaxing genre, slice of life anime generally depict stories of everyday life. Some may find this genre boring since it is more realistic and light, but that is exactly the point. Examples include Barakamon and Hyouka

Mecha - anime where characters pilot giant fucking robots. Needless to say this is one of Japan's most popular exports and will be of interest to anyone that likes to see giant robots beat each other up and destroy literally everything around them. Examples include Darling in the FRANXX and Eureka 7

Horror - a super easy genre to spot. If you scream, wet yourself, or jump out of your seat while watching then it's probably horror. Examples include Hellsing Ultimate and Parasyte

Psychological - uncomfortable to watch at times, anime from this genre like to mess with your head and make you question the very fibre of your being. Examples include Monster and Serial Experiments Lain

Sci-fi - science and technology are central plot elements of these anime and more often than not they are set in space, but can also be set in some obscure and distant future utopian or dystopian society. Examples include From the New World and Legend of the Galactic Heroes

Mystery - anime that fall into this category always have some sort of issue that needs to be solved, or an enigma to be explained. Sherlock Holmes and Scooby Doo fans rejoice. Examples include Eden of the East and The Promised Neverland

Supernatural - anime from this genre can be really strange and often have a paranormal or magical aspect to them. The normal boundaries of reality rarely apply and basically anything can happen. Examples include Mob Psycho 100 and Noragami

Shoujo/ Romance - it's all about love, sweet and tender love. And of course, cute boys and cute girls that just don't know how to tell each other they are head over heels for each other. Examples include Maid Sama! and Lovely Complex

Shounen - anime aimed at young boys. Usually a combination of action and adventure, these shows also tend to have super long runs and are extremely popular even outside the demographic. Examples include One Piece and Fairy Tail

Seinen - a demographic and an anime genre, these anime are aimed at viewers between the ages 18 and 40. Deal with much more mature themes and generally have more complex narratives. Examples include Mushishi and Hinamitsuri

Sports - pretty self-explanatory, but this genre contains anime that depict characters engaging in sports and athletic activities/ competitions. Examples include Baby Steps and Haikyuu!!
